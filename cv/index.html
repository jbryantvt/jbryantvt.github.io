Research Interests                                                                                                                                                        

Plant growth is controlled by dynamic gene expression regulated by environmental stimuli, hormones, cellular history, and other signals. Synthetic biology approaches, where genes are treated as interchangeable devices between organisms, have been used to parse the genetic mechanisms of plant growth in previous work. My objective is to automate synthetic biology through software development and then use this platform to characterize central hormone regulatory systems in plants. Ultimately, I aim to achieve rational control over plant growth to improve agricultural efficiency.

Graduate Research

1.     Spatial and temporal specificity of plant-hormone-induced gene expression and plant development (R Clay Wright, PhD Lab)         2019-

·       Goal: To characterize auxin signaling dynamics and rationally engineer 

gene expression.

·       Approach: Quantify the extent to which binding specificity between

signaling proteins regulates gene expression.

·       We built synthetic variants of auxin signaling proteins to quantify spatial

and temporal transcriptional specificity of these variants on gene

expression with flow cytometry.

·       Expression data will allow us to generate a molecular model that informs

rational engineering of pathway dynamics to control phenotypes

such as apical dominance, phototropism, etc.

 

2.     AssemblyTron: Flexible automation of DNA assembly with Opentrons OT-2 lab robots (R Clay Wright, PhD Lab).                               2020-

·       Github: https://github.com/PlantSynBioLab/AssemblyTron

·       Goal: To automate the error-prone DNA assembly process.

·       Approach: Develop protocols for Opentrons lab robots to assemble plasmids

beginning from primers and DNA templates.

·       This package automatically generates PCR parameters, dilution factors, 

output files, etc. for any DNA construct designed with the J5 algorithm.
