Research Interests

Automated cloning workflows and modular DNA parts enable synthetic biologists to engineer specialized molecular systems for chemical synthesis, environmental response, pharmaceutical manufacturing, etc. I build software packages for automating synthetic biology with liquid handling robotics. I also design custom DNA parts that integrate with existing modular cloning toolkits to facilitate rapid multigene plasmid assembly. With these paired approaches, I am expediting synthetic biology design cycles, improving laboratory throughput, reducing training demands, and promoting reproducibility. I have four years of experience using automation and synthetic biology approaches to study the auxin signaling pathway in plants.

Graduate Research

1.       Spatial and temporal specificity of plant-hormone-induced gene expression and plant development 2019-

         Goal: To characterize auxin signaling dynamics and rationally engineer gene expression.

         Approach: Quantify the extent to which binding specificity between signaling proteins regulates gene

expression.

         We built synthetic variants of auxin signaling proteins to quantify transcriptional specificity of these variants

on gene expression.

         Expression data will allow us to generate a molecular model that informs rational engineering of pathway

dynamics.

 

2.       AssemblyTron: Flexible automation of DNA assembly with Opentrons OT-2 lab robots 2020-

         Github: https://github.com/PlantSynBioLab/AssemblyTron

         Goal: To automate the error-prone DNA assembly process.

         Approach: Develop protocols for Opentrons lab robots to assemble plasmids beginning from primers and

DNA templates.

         This package automatically generates PCR parameters, dilution factors, output files, etc. for any DNA

construct designed with the J5 algorithm.

 

3.       Expediting rootstock and scion breeding for cold and drought tolerance by predicting hormone signaling interactions 2022-

         Goal: To develop high throughput molecular assays for functional characterization of hormone

perception in apple.

         Approach: Infiltrate Abscisic acid response element reporter into apple leaves.

         We have developed an effective agroinfiltration protocol for transient expression of reporters in

apple leaves.
