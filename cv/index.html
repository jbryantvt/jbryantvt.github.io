    Graduate Research

    Spatial and temporal specificity of plant-hormone-induced gene expression and plant development (R Clay Wright, PhD Lab, July 2019-present)

    ·       Goal: To characterize auxin signaling dynamics and rationally engineer gene expression.

    ·       Approach: Quantify the extent to which binding specificity between key signaling proteins regulate gene expression.

    ·       Built synthetic protein interaction variants of auxin signaling proteins and characterized spatial and temporal transcriptional specificity of the synthetic variants on gene expression using flow cytometry.

    ·       Expression data will allow us to generate a molecular model which will inform rational engineering of the pathway.

    ·       Ability to rationally engineer phenotypes such as apical dominance, canopy development, root architecture, etc. will serve as tools to increase agricultural efficiency as population growth and climate fluctuations command higher productivity.

     

    DNtegrate: an open-source python package to integrate DNA vector design and assembly using the OT-2 liquid handling robot (R Clay Wright, PhD Lab; March 2020-present)

    Github: https://github.com/PlantSynBioLab/opentrons

    ·       Goal: To automate the error-prone DNA assembly cloning process.

    ·       Approach: Develop an opensource python package to integrate Opentrons liquid handling API with DNA construct design files.

    ·       This package will automatically generate PCR parameters, dilution factors, output files, etc. for any DNA construct designed with the J5 algorithm.

    ·       Code is being tested with a variety of DNA construct designs in the Plant Synthetic Biology lab and is nearing release.

    Undergraduate Research

    Environmental DNA extraction for development of a noninvasive bat presence assay (May 2018-May 2019)

    ·       Goal: To develop an assay for identifying bat species that been in contact with a body of water.

    ·       This assay would provide a more efficient approach for bat detection than classical survey techniques.

    ·       Collected water samples from ponds, streams, and lakes near bat survey sites in Tennessee.

    ·       Optimized extraction technique for collecting environmental DNA from water samples.

    ·       Currently developing a general primer for regional bat species to analyze the extracted eDNA.

     

    Impacts of a chicken processing plant on water quality and bacteria concentrations in an adjacent wetland and creek (In the lab of Elizabeth Dobbins, PhD, August 2016-May 2018)

    ·       Determined that elevated conductivity, E. coli concentrations, and lower DO could be attributed to effluent discharge from the Tyson chicken processing plan due to presence of chicken fecal bacteria in water samples.

    ·       Collected samples from Graves Creek (tributary to the Black Warrior River) and an adjacent marsh surrounding a Tyson chicken processing plant.

    ·       Recorded in situ pH, dissolved oxygen (DO), and conductivity readings at sample sites.

    ·       Recorded E. coli presence in cfu/mL based on wetland and creek samples cultured on Coliscan MF pads.

    ·       Performed PCR microbial source tracking on DNA samples from the creek.
